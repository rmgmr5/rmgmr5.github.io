Projects

Photo

For the first imaging assignment, we were tasked to create unique banner and icon images, with an overall theme of positive parenting. In order to create said images, I used Adobe Photoshop to crop and collage various stock photos. The process was not too difficult as it was mainly composed of the fundamental techniques of digital imaging. Finding my way around the software turned out to be very intuitive, which ultimately helped me to create the finished products with ease. The second imaging assignment was a bit more challenging. For this one, we had to seamlessly integrate one photo into another; and although the underlying idea was somewhat similar to the first assignment, the execution was much different. I decided to take two original photos that I had taken on my phone. The first was a photo of a valley I took in Colorado, and the second was a photo of myself in a St. Louis book store. From here, I attempted to place myself into the Coloradan valley. I made sure to cut myself out with great detail, so as to preserve the illusion I wanted to create. After pasting myself into the new photo, I tweaked the filters and color grading to achieve the seamless look.
